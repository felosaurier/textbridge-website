The TextBridge Story

From a simple idea to a revolutionary solution - discover how TextBridge came to life.

The Beginning

TextBridge began as a simple observation: communication barriers faced by deaf individuals and international travelers are significant yet often overlooked. In 2025, three students at HTL Ungargasse‚ÄîFelix Horngacher, Oliver Kellner, and Raphael Klein‚Äîdecided to tackle this challenge for their diploma project.

What started as a classroom discussion evolved into a comprehensive project that combines artificial intelligence, hardware engineering, and user-centered design to create a solution that could truly make a difference in people's lives.

Project Timeline

September 2025

The Spark

During a brainstorming session at HTL Ungargasse, the team identified the need for better assistive technology for the deaf community. After researching existing solutions, they realized there was an opportunity to create something truly innovative.

October 2025

Project Proposal

The team formally proposed TextBridge as their diploma project. Under the mentorship of Dipl.-Ing. Mag. Dr. Martin Hasitschka, they outlined the project scope, objectives, and timeline. The proposal was approved, and the journey officially began.

November 2025

Research & Development

Extensive research into speech recognition, sign language processing, and wearable technology began. The team studied existing STT models, with Felix leading the exploration of the Vosk model for accurate speech recognition. They also conducted user interviews with members of the deaf community to understand their needs.

December 2025

First Prototype

Oliver developed the first hardware prototype‚Äîa basic glasses frame with an integrated display. While rudimentary, it proved the concept was viable and provided valuable insights into the design challenges ahead.

January 2026

AI Model Training

Felix successfully trained the initial speech-to-text model using the Vosk framework. The team also began developing the sign language recognition algorithms, creating custom datasets and training neural networks for gesture recognition.

February 2026

Software Integration

Raphael developed the companion mobile application and integrated the AI models with the hardware. The team faced challenges with real-time processing but overcame them through optimization and efficient architecture design.

March 2026

Translation Feature

Recognizing the potential for international applications, the team added multi-language translation capabilities. This expanded TextBridge's utility beyond the deaf community to include travelers and international professionals.

April 2026

User Testing

Beta testing began with volunteers from the deaf community and language learners. Feedback was overwhelmingly positive, though users provided valuable suggestions for improvements in accuracy, comfort, and battery life.

May 2026

Refinement & Iteration

Based on user feedback, the team refined the design, improved the AI models, and enhanced the user interface. Oliver redesigned the glasses for better ergonomics, while Felix optimized the algorithms for improved accuracy and speed.

June 2026

Final Prototype

The final prototype was completed, incorporating all feedback and improvements. The team conducted extensive testing to ensure reliability, accuracy, and user satisfaction. The device exceeded their initial expectations.

July - December 2026

Documentation & Presentation

The team prepared comprehensive documentation, including technical specifications, user manuals, and research findings. They also created presentations and demonstration videos for their diploma defense.

January 2027

Project Launch

TextBridge was officially launched with the completion of the diploma project. The team successfully defended their work and received recognition for their innovative approach to accessibility technology.

Challenges We Overcame

‚ö° Real-Time Processing

Challenge: Processing speech and sign language in real-time required significant computational power, which was difficult to achieve in a compact, wearable device.

Solution: The team optimized their algorithms and implemented edge computing techniques, allowing most processing to occur on the device itself rather than relying on cloud services.

üîã Battery Life

Challenge: Initial prototypes had limited battery life, lasting only 2-3 hours‚Äîinsufficient for all-day use.

Solution: Oliver redesigned the power management system and selected more efficient components, extending battery life to 8 hours while maintaining performance.

üéØ Accuracy

Challenge: Achieving high accuracy in speech and sign language recognition across different accents, dialects, and signing styles was complex.

Solution: Felix expanded the training datasets and implemented adaptive learning algorithms that improve with use, learning from individual users' speech patterns and signing styles.

üëì Comfort & Design

Challenge: Creating glasses that were comfortable for extended wear while housing all necessary technology was a significant design challenge.

Solution: Multiple design iterations and user testing led to an ergonomic design that distributes weight evenly and uses lightweight materials without compromising functionality.

TextBridge represents more than just a diploma project‚Äîit's a step toward a more inclusive world. By breaking down communication barriers, we enable deaf individuals to participate more fully in education, work, and social life.
